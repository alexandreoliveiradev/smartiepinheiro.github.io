Trees and tents
There are 12 trees on a campsite. You, as a campsite manager, must find a spot for each tent that meets the following requirements:
1. In total there are as many tents as trees. So every tent has its own tree.
2. A tree must be immediately next to each tent (diagonal is not allowed).
3. A tent can make contact with multiple trees, but is only connected to one.
4. Tents never touch each other: neither horizontally nor vertically nor diagonally.
5. The numbers outside indicate how many tents there are in that row/column.
Click once to make the cell green if you believe a tent can't be placed there.
Click a second time to place a tent. And a third to go back to a blank.
